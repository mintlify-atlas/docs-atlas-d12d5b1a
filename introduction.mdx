---

JSBSim computes the forces and moments acting on a vehicle and integrates the full nonlinear equations of motion at each simulation step. You configure every subsystem — aerodynamics, propulsion, flight control, landing gear — through XML files, and drive the simulation through a C++ API, a Python module, a MATLAB S-Function, or an Unreal Engine plugin.

## Key capabilities

- **Nonlinear 6-DoF dynamics** — Full equations of motion with Coriolis and centrifugal acceleration modeled.
- **Accurate Earth model** — WGS84 oblate spheroid geometry, geodetic coordinates, and the International Standard Atmosphere (ISA 1976).
- **XML-driven configuration** — Aircraft geometry, aerodynamics, propulsion, flight control systems, and simulation scripts are all defined in human-readable XML.
- **Configurable flight control system** — PID controllers, actuators, filters, sensors, switches, and schedulers connected as a signal-flow graph.
- **Rich propulsion models** — Piston, turbine, turboprop, rocket, electric, and rotor models.
- **Flexible data output** — Write simulation state to screen, CSV files, sockets, or the FlightGear native protocol.
- **Standalone or embedded** — Run as a console-mode batch simulator or embed it inside another application.

## Language bindings

JSBSim exposes its full feature set through three official bindings in addition to the native C++ API.

| Binding | Distribution |
|---|---|
| **Python** | Binary wheels on PyPI (`pip install jsbsim`) and conda-forge |
| **MATLAB / Simulink** | S-Function shipped with the Windows installer and source tree |
| **Unreal Engine** | Plugin in the `UnrealEngine/` directory of the repository |

## Real-world usage

JSBSim is used across flight simulation, autopilot development, and AI research.

- **FlightGear**, **OutTerra**, and **Skybolt** use JSBSim as their primary physics engine.
- **ArduPilot** and **PX4 Autopilot** use JSBSim for Software-In-The-Loop (SITL) drone testing.
- **DARPA's AlphaDogfight Trials** featured a JSBSim-backed agent that defeated a graduate of the U.S. Air Force's F-16 Weapons Instructor Course in match play.
- **NASA** verified JSBSim against six in-house simulation tools and found agreement across all published test cases.
- **Unreal Engine's Antoinette Project** uses JSBSim to power next-generation flight simulator tooling.

JSBSim also has more than 1,000 citations in academic literature (Google Scholar, as of May 2025) and is licensed under the LGPL 2.1.
